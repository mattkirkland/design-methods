How do I research users? And why?

We'll cover: Intro to design research. Why do designers research? what kinds of things qualify as research?

Ethnographic Research

This week we'll focus on a subset of research methods: ethnographic research. Find out what your users do now! We'll do a light touch overview of ethnographic research models. We'll cover:

  • popular media scan *
  • literature / niche reading *
  • interest groups *
  • field study
  • contextual inquiry
  • desirepath/postit/annotations
  • diary studies *
  • interviews
  • SME interview

Assignment

    For Tuesday September 22 (by 8am as always):
  • Make a new page in your Notion notebook.
  • Choose a flavor of ethnographic research; do it and report findings in your playground notebook. Remember: you are trying to find out how people solve the problem, or use the thing, right now - before you do any new design work.
  • IMPORTANT NOTE: you must FIND users and TALK to them. That means for this exercise you cannot use the methods marked with an * above, unless there's an IRL knitting circle or Discord channel that you can join for the 'interest group' immersion. Diary Study is probably also not going to work given your time constraint.
