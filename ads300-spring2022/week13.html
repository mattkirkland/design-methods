Are there other design methodologies I should be considering?

You betcha

Methods vs Methodology. Discuss.

Thus far everything we've done has been rooted in a user-centered, iterative design process. But there are other frameworks or methodologies that designers use! You may be interested in how these apply to your work, and you might dive in further or become a religious adherent to one or more.

Human-centered Design vs User-centered Design. Design Thinking. Activity-centered Design. Process-Centered Design. Contextual Design. Participatory Design. Universal Design. Transgenerational Design. Service Design. Evidence-based Design. Thanatosensitivity. Metadesign. Lean Design. Agile Design. Generative Design. Machine Learning.

Lots of these start out as marketing for a specific design firm or designer or researcher. That doesn't make them bullshit necessarily!
